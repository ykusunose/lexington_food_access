Are there 'food deserts' in Lexington?

Food deserts are areas where residents lack access to an adequate variety and quantity of food products, particularly fresh fruits and vegetables. Geographic distance to food retailers matters, but so do the income and transportation capacity of households. Higher-income households are more likely to be able to regularly access the desired quantity, quality, and variety of food they desire, no matter where they live. Lower-income households, however, are more constrained by issues of transportation and price. For these households, having a full-service food retailer (e.g., a supermarket) located nearby likely increases food security.

This map

This map shows the median income of households, by ZIP code, as well as the location of full-service food retailers, including Kroger, Save-A-Lot, Walmart, Aldi, and Meijer. Click on the red dots for the retailer name. It excludes ethnic food retailers (e.g., YuYu and Supermercado Aguascalientes) simply due to the author's unfamiliarity with all such retailers and their offerings.

Data and tools

Supermarket locations were digitized by the author in QGIS3.12 using locations identified in Google Maps with the search term "grocery store." Income data (collected 2006-2010 via the American Community Survey) were downloaded from the University of Michigan Population Studies Center. ZIP code shapefiles were downloaded from the data.gov website. These data were loaded onto CARTO, which hosts the map above.
